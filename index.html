Preflight checks for risky production changes.

PreflightOps turns a service catalog and a proposed change into a clear 0–100 risk score, a risk level, and an actionable list of the exact gaps to fix — before the change ships.

Runs locally as a Streamlit app, a CLI, and a GitHub Action. No database, no login, no AI — just transparent, auditable rules. Outbound calls only when you opt in to ServiceNow / Jira.

The problem

Most incidents start as risky changes.

Teams invest heavily in CI/CD, Terraform, Kubernetes, and observability — yet the decision "is this change safe to ship?" is still made by gut feel in a rushed review.

Rollback plans are missing, vague, or untested.

Critical services lack runbooks or a named owner.

Monitoring and post-deploy validation are afterthoughts.

Risky Terraform and Kubernetes changes slip through review.

PreflightOps makes that judgement explicit, consistent, and auditable. It encodes change-governance and production-readiness checks as transparent rules, so every change gets the same scrutiny — and reviewers can focus on the findings that actually matter.

How it works

Four steps from change to verdict.

01

Describe your service

Point PreflightOps at a service catalog: ownership, criticality, runbooks, monitoring, and rollback expectations.

02

Describe the change

Declare what's shipping — environment, change type, and the rollback, monitoring, and validation plans behind it.

03

Scan infrastructure signals

Parse Terraform plans and Kubernetes manifests for risky IAM, destroy actions, exposed Secrets, and missing probes.

04

Comment on the pull request

Get a 0–100 score, a risk level, and the exact gaps to fix — posted straight onto the PR, optionally blocking the merge.

GitHub Action

Gate every pull request.

Drop this into .github/workflows/preflight.yml and PreflightOps reviews every change automatically — failing the check when the risk crosses your threshold.

preflight.yml 
name: PreflightOps
on: [pull_request]

jobs:
  preflight:
    runs-on: ubuntu-latest
    steps:
      - uses: actions/checkout@v4
      - uses: pedroluna-gh/preflightops@v0.1.0
        with:
          services: services.yaml
          change: change.yaml
          terraform: tfplan.txt
          k8s: k8s.yaml
          fail-on: critical

Risk signals

Every check, on every change.

Transparent rules across governance and infrastructure — each one contributes to the final score.

Service ownership

Flags services with no named owner, missing runbooks, or undefined business impact.

Rollback readiness

Catches production changes shipping without a valid, tested rollback plan.

Monitoring coverage

Detects incomplete monitoring plans so regressions don't go unnoticed.

Validation plan

Checks for a post-deploy validation plan that proves the change actually worked.

Business criticality

Weighs risk by how critical the service is to the business and its customers.

Terraform risk

Scans plans for IAM/role changes, security groups, DNS, KMS, DB instances, and destroy actions.

Kubernetes risk

Spots exposed Secrets, Ingress/LoadBalancer changes, replicas: 0, and missing readiness/liveness probes.

ServiceNow / Jira

From PR check to change ticket.

Every assessment produces a copy/paste-ready change summary for ServiceNow, Jira, or a CAB review — and, when you opt in, pushes it straight into a real change record. Offline by default; outbound only when you ask for it.

change-summary.md 
# Production Change Summary

## Change Title
Upgrade checkout service to v2.4

## Service
checkout-api

## Environment
production

## Risk Level
HIGH

## Risk Score
72/100

## Required Approvals
Senior engineering or platform review recommended before deployment.

## Rollback Plan
Re-deploy v2.3 image; feature flag checkout_v2 off.

## Recommended Deployment Window
Use a low-traffic window with rollback owner and monitoring confirmed.

Copy/paste Markdown summary

Every assessment renders a structured change record — risk level, score, approvals, rollback, and findings — ready to paste into a ServiceNow or Jira ticket.

Opt-in live push

Choose to push the same summary straight into ServiceNow or Jira from the CLI, GitHub Action, or web app — creating or updating a real change record.

Custom templates

Tailor the layout to your change-management format — section order, headings, and approval wording — with a simple YAML or JSON template. No code changes.

Offline by default

No network calls, tokens, or external services unless you explicitly opt in. Credentials come from the environment only — outbound, never inbound.
